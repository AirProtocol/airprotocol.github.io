About AirProtocol

A blockchain-based protocol for peer-to-peer exchange would enable trust-minimized and censorship-resistant free market exchange of fiat money for cryptocurrency, with offchain performance (i.e. fiat transfer) derisked by a multi-signature escrow of onchain collateral. Decentralized public blockchains provide the foundation for such a protocol because cryptocurrency transfers are difficult to censor, public, and irreversible. Peer-to-peer exchange platforms have proven less susceptible to state censorship than local centralized exchanges (and can support clients globally), but their utility for any fiat crypto pair depends on a secure and active market of exchange requestors and acceptors in each supported country. AirProtocol is an open source platform for forming trust-minimized exchange markets. A system of smart contracts enables the permissionless request and acceptance of fiat crypto exchange, as well as the escrow and collateral that derisks offchain performance.

How Does AirProtocol Change Things?

Existing centralized solutions for peer-to-peer exchange fail to address the needs of many markets.

Centralized

Single Points of Failure
  • Governments can impose foreign exchange controls.
  • Large and powerful financial institutions can collude against clients.
  • Monetary and financial authorities are not held accountable.

AirProtocol

Censorship Resistance
  • Decentralized communities are difficult to hold accountable.
  • Financial service providers have individual freedom and accountability.
  • Authority is distributed among the network participants.

Centralized

Closed Innovation
  • Financial networks become fragmented as businesses adopt new ideas.
  • Innovators have to consider and react to market demand.
  • Capital allocation decisions are delegated to small groups of individuals.

AirProtocol

Collaborative Extensibility
  • The network expands and becomes more comprehensive as contributors introduce new ideas.
  • Market participants directly drive innovation.
  • All stakeholders are incentivized to vote with their capital.

Centralized

Restricted Offerings
  • Clients are restricted to one service provider for many useful transactions.
  • Privacy protections are dictated by local regulations or industry standards.
  • Businesses facilitate their own communities of clients and employees.

AirProtocol

Freedom of Choice
  • Service providers are granular and interoperable with each other.
  • Users can choose the privacy protections that best suit their needs.
  • Communities and networks are self-organizing.
